Tokyo, Japan

Born and raised in Tokyo, Japan, I grew up playing sports and exploring the concrete jungle. Thinking back, having lived in Tokyo was a big reason why I became to love computer technology. Back then, I was always curious how train systems in Japan are so reliable and consistently on-time. Tokyo is a big part of who I am today.

Portland, Oregon USA

April 7th, 2006, my family and I moved to Portland, Oregon. I barely spoke English and when I did, I had a thick accent. No doubt, I was scared shitless (excuse me, but it's true). I couldn't communicate, and the language barrier was like no other. Having been able to communicate all your life and not knowing how to in the U.S., it was like working on a big project and coming across an error you don't recognize: You don't know how to patch it or improvise. It's frustrating and stressful.

Portland is full of interesting people and it has a unique culture. (Though, personally, it rains too much). Again, Portland is a big part of who I am today.

P.S. If you are thinking of moving to Portland, don't. It's a crummy place (Portlanders will understand why I say this)

Seoul, South Korea

Skipping time a tad bit, in Fall 2014 I started school at Korea University in Anam-dong, Seoul. Aside from meeting cool people and making international friends, I got to emerse myself in omnipresent Free public wifi! (Which was more reliable than my uni. wifi... That's amazing, but sad at the same time).

As a student abroad, I wanted to know how they teach programming in their higher education. Nothing special nor different from the U.S. BUT Korean students are more talented overall. This motivated me to start a project and spend more time practicing code.

In my opinion, traveling makes you smarter because you constantly surround yourself with worldviews. More importantly, you actively put yourself in an inferior position, which makes you think empathetically. To me, this is important because being empathetic (whether solving problems or designing) forces you to think creatively. Spending money on experiences like traveling is better than material goods because it's not something you can search on Google.

TipsyTravels.com was a forum I started in attempt to share this philosophy.

Denver, Colorado USA

I'm not the type of person who finds rain to be therapeutic. For me, traveling is therapeutic and the best feeling is when I'm on a plane knowing I will land somewhere new.

I love the sun and bouldering, so Denver was a perfect location for me.

Currently attending Regis University in Denver, I'm majoring in Computer Science and minoring in Math. (Graduating on the 1st of May). I enjoy working collaboratively and making functional software that solves a problem creatively. I especially enjoy brainstorming ideas because it's stimulating and inspirational.

Intrinsically motivated, I love learning new technologies, from software development to cooking, cultures to web UX design. My recent youtube therapy has been learning about Molecular Gastronomy, which is oddly satisfying to see and fun to learn.
